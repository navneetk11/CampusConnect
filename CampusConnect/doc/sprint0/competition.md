Existing Products:

* Study Buddy \- [https://study-buddy.co/](https://study-buddy.co/)  
  * A global study network where individuals can connect worldwide with other individuals, one-on-one, to study  
  * Study sessions are structured and focused  
  * Statistics such as completion rates and productivity trends are tracked 	

* Study Together \- [https://www.studytogether.com/](https://www.studytogether.com/)  
  * Global study network where virtual group study rooms can be created and joined  
  * Personal goals can be customized and displayed  
  * Study statistics are tracked  
  * Tutors are available for assistance

How does our product differ?

* Study groups will be more localized, connecting individuals enrolled in York University (rather than on a global scale)  
* Study groups may meet in person, rather than purely virtual  
* Groups will be able to be filtered by common courses/program, making it easier to connect with like-minded individuals learning the same concepts as you are  
* Overall, this product is better for:  
  * York University students struggling to grasp concepts/ study effectively within their courses   
  * York University students looking to meet like-minded individuals to motivate efficient and productive work

* Why didn't competitors prioritize this audience/goal?  
  * The priority for these competitors seems to be connecting people on a worldwide scale that may not have local study partners  
  * If they did prioritize students at York, the scope of their application would be much smaller than intended  
  * Additionally, they would have to introduce many specific features (that wouldn't apply to all of their intended audience), such as in-person meetup planning, school schedule integration, course code filtration, etc.
