# PROCESS REFLECTION

**TEAM ORGANIZATION**

During Sprint 0, our team organized work collaboratively rather than assigning rigid roles. Responsibilities were divided based on interest and availability, allowing each member to contribute meaningfully to discussions, documentation, and planning. While roles were flexible, team members naturally focused on areas such as documentation, feature definition, and user experience planning.

We used the following tools to support collaboration:

* ***Google Meet*** for synchronous discussions and major decision-making  
* ***Discord*** for ongoing communication and updates  
* ***Google Docs*** for shared drafting, editing, and proofreading  
* ***GitHub*** for version control and final submission  
* ***Trello*** to organize user stories and track progress

This structure allowed us to work efficiently while maintaining transparency and shared ownership.

**DECISION MAKING**

Key decisions were made through structured discussions during Google Meet sessions. We met twice to finalize the project idea, scope, and core deliverables. During these meetings, each team member shared their perspective before the group reached consensus.

Between meetings, decisions were refined through Discord discussions. We prioritized clear communication and ensured that no major decision was finalized unless all team members were comfortable with the outcome. This approach helped maintain alignment and avoided misunderstandings.

**PRIORITIZING USER STORIES**

User stories were prioritized based on their importance to the core functionality of the platform. We categorized features into:

* ***Core features*** that are essential for the primary workflow. This includes core functionality such as account creation, course 	   filtering, and the ability to discover and join study groups. Without these features, the system would not function as intended.  
* ***Secondary features*** that enhance the overall user experience but are not required for the platform’s basic operation. Examples      include file uploads within group chats and the calendar view for study sessions.  
* ***Stretch features*** to be implemented if time permits

Prioritization was guided by alignment with key usage scenarios, technical feasibility, and dependencies between features. On average, one to two rounds of discussion were needed to reach consensus on priority and scope.

**MEETINGS AND COMMUNICATION**

Our team held two formal Google Meet meetings during Sprint 0 to discuss project direction and finalize documentation. Outside of these meetings, we communicated regularly through Discord to share updates, ask questions, and clarify tasks.

We used shared Google Docs to allow all members to contribute, edit, and proofread documents collaboratively before committing finalized versions to GitHub. This ensured clarity, consistency, and collective ownership of all deliverables.

**NEXT PHASE & LESSONS LEARNED**

For Sprint 1, we have identified several key lessons to carry forward:

* ***Technical Connectivity***  
  We will prioritize early integration between the frontend and backend to ensure that the MVC architecture is robust before adding more complex features. Early connectivity will reduce integration risks later in the sprint.  
* ***Communication***  
  We found that prompt responses on Discord are critical for meeting deadlines. Moving forward, we will strictly adhere to the contact methods listed in team.md to ensure accountability and timely coordination.  
* ***Documentation and Task Tracking***  
  We will keep our Trello board updated in real time to accurately reflect task progress. Maintaining up-to-date documentation will help avoid last-minute work and improve sprint planning.

Overall, Sprint 0 allowed us to establish strong communication practices, align on project scope, and identify improvements to carry into development. These lessons position our team well for a smoother and more structured Sprint 1\.
